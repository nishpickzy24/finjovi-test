Stocks

Stocks, also known as shares or equities, represent ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a part-owner of that company and gain the right to share in its profits, usually through dividends or stock price appreciation. Stocks are traded on stock exchanges like the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) or NASDAQ, where investors buy and sell them based on market demand. Investing in stocks is one of the most popular ways to build wealth over time, but it also comes with risks, as stock prices can fluctuate due to company performance, economic conditions, and global event

Companies list their stocks in the market to raise capital. This money is used for expansion, research, paying debts, or developing new products. Instead of taking loans, they offer ownership to the public. In return, investors get a chance to share in the company‚Äôs success.

Stock prices change constantly based on supply and demand. If many people want to buy a stock, its price goes up. If more people want to sell, the price goes down.
